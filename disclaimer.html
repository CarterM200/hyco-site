1. Consult a professional first

Always seek the advice of a qualified healthcare provider before beginning, changing or intensifying any exercise or nutrition programme — and especially if you: are pregnant or postpartum; are under 18; have or suspect a heart condition, high blood pressure, diabetes, an injury, an eating disorder, or any chronic or acute medical condition; are recovering from illness or surgery; or take medication that could be affected by exercise or diet. You are solely responsible for exercising within your own limits, using correct technique, warming up appropriately, and choosing whether any session, load or pace is suitable for you on the day.

3. Listen to your body — stop and seek help

Stop exercising immediately and seek medical attention if you experience chest pain, severe shortness of breath, dizziness, faintness, palpitations, severe or unusual pain, or any other warning sign. In an emergency, call 999 (or your local emergency number). Do not wait for, or rely on, the app.
